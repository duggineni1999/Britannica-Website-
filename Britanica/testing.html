Monumental Heights

This weekend marks the anniversary of two iconic American landmarks, one a towering presence in New York harbor and the other a gateway on the shores of the Mississippi River.
Statue of Liberty
The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France, designed by sculptor Frédéric-Auguste Bartholdi in 1875. The colossus was presented to the American minister in a ceremony on July 4, 1884. The next year, the completed statue—151 feet 1 inch (46 meters) high and weighing 225 tons—was disassembled, shipped to New York City, and rebuilt. It was dedicated by Pres. Grover Cleveland on Oct. 28, 1886.
Gateway Arch
This landmark takes its name from the role of St. Louis, Mo., as the “Gateway to the West” during the U.S. expansion of the 19th century. Designed by architect Eero Saarinen in 1948, the arch is 630 feet (192 meters) tall, and the distance between its two legs is equal to its height. Completed on Oct. 28, 1965, the Gateway Arch commemorates the Louisiana Purchase and subsequent opening of the West to settlers.
